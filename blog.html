Our Journey

Explore the milestones and achievements that have shaped Luna Picker into the revolutionary product it is today.

Step 1

As the basic step of our bot, wheels, we customized the two rear wheels ourselves and produced it at a 3D printer shop. That wheels are wrapped with rubber bands to prevent them from slipping in smooth surfaces

Creating 3D printer wheel

Step 2

After wheels, we starts to build the body of our robot. We hack the aluminium rods as cross for wheels installation at the base of the acrylic frame. At the top, we will put the arm, the container and the circuit room. On the button part, we put the motors and wheels for moving bots. At the top of the cross-hacked Arcylic frame,we divide two parts again. At the top part, we put the robotics arm and container.We make the room for circuit for avoiding the damage of circuit.We connect the two frames by using stands.

Step 3

We make the small room for circuit structure to avoid damage. We cut the aluminium rod for pulling the aluminium flat as the open and close parts of the room.

Step 4

On the base, we configure the 3D printed wheels as the front wheels and JGA 25-370 motors with wheels as the backward wheels. All four wheels are detained to the frame by using block bearings.The The combination of wheels on the bot moves forward, backward, left, right and even drift.

Testing for movement

Step 5

We configure these wheels with power supply as two slot container and four slot containers connected to circuit at the circuit room. Inside the room, we set Arduino Uno board connecting with breadboard, jumper wire and power supply, L298N module and cooling fan for reducing heat in this circuit room.

Step 6

The switch is placed outside the circuit protection room for convenient system operation without exposing the internal components.And also a manual horn is added to assist the user in avoiding obstacles by providing alerts

Testing for Horn using My Luna Picker Application

Step 7

We configure the acrylic container on the bot. This container is for placing things that the robotic arm picked. In the front of the bot, we place the robotic arm which is controlled by servo motors

Step 8

At the front of the bot, we put the ESP-32 Web Cam. It is used for seeing the obstacles on our bot way to take a horn and supported with bluetooth connectivity.

Testing for ESP 32 Web Camera

Step 9

After the whole body frame are completed, we start the arm construction. We apply the servo bractes for arm.

Step 10

Arm is ran with 6 servo motors and fix with servo brackets. Servo brackets are used to securely mount and position of servo motors in arm. They provide a stable and adjustable platform for all the servo motor, ensuring precise movement and reducing wear and tear. Our robotics arm has three joints

Testing for Gripper
